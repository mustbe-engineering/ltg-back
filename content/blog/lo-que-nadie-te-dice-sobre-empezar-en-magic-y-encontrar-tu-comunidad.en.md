When people ask me about my journey in Magic: The Gathering, I usually think that it wasn't linear, and it wasn't love at first sight either... until suddenly, it was.

I didn't start by playing MTG. My first introduction to TCGs was as a child, when Yu-Gi-Oh was airing on television. I remember collecting the cards solely because the illustrations seemed beautiful to me, but despite trying to learn it several times and having it explained to me on more than one occasion, I never fully understood how to play it.

It wasn't until many years later, once I was completely immersed in the world of tabletop and role-playing games, that I approached TCGs again. This time it was thanks to my boyfriend and Lorcana: together we went to Mega XP in Mexico City and bought our first decks. It seemed very fun to me and the illustrations were beautiful, but after several months of playing it, I lost interest. I'm not saying it's a bad game—quite the opposite; I simply didn't feel like it was quite for me.

That's when Magic came into our lives through a great friend (shoutout to Fabián), who lent my boyfriend a Lord of the Rings deck. We sat down to play for the first time and I have to admit it intimidated me quite a bit. Unlike other TCGs, Magic is the pioneer of the genre, and the sheer number of cards, terms, effects, and possibilities when building a deck felt frankly overwhelming.

For a while I stayed away. Despite my boyfriend's enthusiasm and his repeated invitations to play, I avoided it. I think it was fear: fear of trying something new, something so dense that I felt could stress me out more than I already was. But after several insistences, I finally gave in. At first, it was exactly as intense as I imagined. My boyfriend would tell me things like "tap five red manas and X four times to activate the effect," and I would stare at him without understanding absolutely anything, my head about to explode. Still, I decided to be patient and keep trying, and little by little something changed. I began learning, progressing, and without realizing it, I fell in love with something I didn't expect: the possibility of completely disconnecting from the world, of focusing on something other than work or everyday problems.

However, as I explored more about the community, it came as no surprise to discover that the physical spaces where TCGs are played in our city are dominated by men. I'm not saying that's inherently bad, but starting a new hobby as a minority in those spaces is quite intimidating, and based on negative past experiences, I learned to avoid them. That's why I simply assumed that this hobby wasn't for me.

That's how I had the opportunity to meet Vanessa, an incredible girl with a unique project in our city: Ladies The Gathering. It seemed extraordinary to find a space where I could play and show myself as I am, without fear of being judged, criticized, or rejected. I've always struggled to make friends; I can count them on one hand and perhaps because of my introverted nature or my tendency toward antisocial behavior, I arrived at that first event a bit defensive, I must admit. I ended up sitting with a girl who had also just started the hobby and I immediately noticed she felt the same as I did: she was afraid to ask questions, afraid that people would get frustrated with her or that the answer would be negative. So I gladly explained what I knew, and I saw how all the other girls took the same approach, with a smile and phrases I still remember: "*We're all here to learn and help each other!*", "*over time you'll teach us*", "*we all started this way*". It was a genuinely beautiful experience.

I got home smiling that night, not stopping to tell my boyfriend how happy I was for having dared to step out of my comfort zone. I felt, for the first time in a long time, that I had the opportunity to make new friends—something that, as I mentioned, had always been very difficult for me.

Finding that place was a true gem. It made me feel, for the first time in a long time, truly welcomed and accepted. I felt that I had found my safe space.

Since becoming part of this community, I have grown a lot as a player and also as a person. This project has changed many things in me, and I'm sure it has for many of the girls who participate as well. I've confirmed this because they've told me themselves. It's beautiful to see how we all support each other with the best attitude and continue growing together.

If you ever feel like trying to play, don't be afraid to take that first step. Don't be afraid to try. And if at some point you feel like there's no space where you fit, keep looking: I thought the same thing, and it turned out that place did exist. It was just waiting for me to be brave enough to find it.
